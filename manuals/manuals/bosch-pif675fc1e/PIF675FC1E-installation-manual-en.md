## Important notes

### Safety

Safety in use is guaranteed only when the hob has been installed correctly and in accordance with the assembly instructions. The installer is liable for damage caused by unsuitable installation.

After installation, make sure that the user cannot access electrical components.

The appliance is permitted for use at elevations up to 4,000 m above sea level.

### Wearers of electronic implants

The appliance may contain permanent magnets that can affect electronic implants, such as pacemakers or insulin pumps. During installation, wearers of electronic implants must keep at least 10 cm away from the appliance.

### Electrical connection

Electrical connection may be carried out only by an authorised specialist. Observe the requirements of the local electricity supplier.

The appliance must be connected to a fixed installation with appropriate isolating switches installed in accordance with the installation regulations.

The appliance is protection class I and may be operated only with a grounding-conductor connection.

The manufacturer accepts no responsibility for malfunction or damage caused by an incorrect electrical installation.

### Power cable

The power cable may already be connected to the connection box on the hob or may be supplied with the appliance. It may be installed only by an authorised specialist or qualified after-sales service technician. The required connection data is shown on the rating plate and the connection diagram.

Use only the cable supplied with the appliance or one supplied by technical after-sales service. If a longer cable is required, contact after-sales service; connection cables up to 2.20 m are available.

### Permitted installation positions

The induction hob may be installed only above drawers or a fan-ventilated oven. Do not install a refrigerator, dishwasher, washing machine, or an oven without ventilation below the hob.

The clearance between the extraction hood and the hob must be at least the distance specified in the hood installation instructions.

The worktop must be flat, horizontal, stable, and installed according to its manufacturer's instructions. It must support a load of approximately 60 kg. If necessary, reinforce it with temperature- and water-resistant material. Check that the hob is level only after it has been installed.

An unsuitable installation, connection, or assembly invalidates the product warranty. Changes inside the appliance, including replacement of the power cable, may be performed only by specially trained Technical Assistance Service personnel.

## Preparing the cupboard - figures 1, 2, and 3

- Built-in kitchen units must resist temperatures of at least 90 °C.
- Remove all shavings after cutting the opening.
- Seal cut surfaces with heat-resistant material.
- Use the original PDF drawings for all cut-out dimensions and edge clearances.

## Assembly above a drawer - figure 2a

- The worktop must be at least 16 mm thick.
- The clearance between the worktop surface and the upper area of the drawer must be 65 mm.
- If metal objects in the drawer could become hot from circulating air, or if small pointed objects such as skewers are stored there, fit a wooden intermediate bottom as shown in figure 10.
- A fan is located on the underside of the hob. If no intermediate bottom is fitted, do not store small or pointed objects, paper, or kitchen cloths in the drawer. They may obstruct cooling or be drawn into and damage the fan.
- Keep at least 2 cm between the drawer contents and the fan inlet.

## Assembly above an oven - figure 2b

- The worktop must be at least 16 mm thick.
- Keep 30 mm between the worktop surface and the top of the oven front.
- If the hob-to-oven distance must be increased, consult the oven installation instructions.
- Keep at least 5 mm between the oven and the hob for ventilation.
- If the oven is made by another manufacturer, use a wooden separation board as shown in figure 10.

## Ventilation - figure 3

The hob requires adequate fresh-air ventilation through the lower part of the appliance. The cupboard must provide:

- the clearances shown between the rear of the cupboard and the kitchen wall, and between the worktop and the upper area of the drawer (figure 3a);
- an opening at the top rear of the cupboard (figure 3b);
- an underside opening if the minimum 20 mm clearance at the rear of the cupboard cannot be provided;
- suitable alternative ventilation when installed in an island or another configuration not shown in the manual.

Do not hermetically seal the lower base.

## Installing the appliance - figures 4-9

Wear protective gloves. Concealed surfaces may have sharp edges.

For appliances without a pre-installed cable:

1. Turn the hob over and place it on its packaging base, a cloth, or another surface that prevents scratches.
2. Lift the connection-box cover with a screwdriver (figure 4).
3. Undo the mounting screw and lift the cable clamp with a screwdriver (figure 5).
4. Connect only as shown in the connection-box diagram (figure 6). If diagram `2L/2N~` is used, change the bridge arrangement exactly as shown. Tighten all connection-box screws after connecting the conductors.
5. Secure the power cable with the cable clamp and tighten the retaining screw (figure 7a).
6. Close the connection-box cover. Arrange the conductors in the centre of the box so that it closes correctly.
7. If the cable interferes with the drawer, secure it to the underside of the hob with the supplied cable ties (figure 7b).

Install the hob:

1. Fit the mounting rails. Remove previously fitted rails and install the new rails. Use the lower threaded holes for tiled worktops. On stone worktops, bond the rails with a heat-resistant adhesive suitable for metal and stone.
2. Push the appliance into the installation opening. Do not trap the cable or route it over sharp edges. With a built-under oven, route the cable around the rear corners of the oven to the connection box so that it cannot touch hot parts of either appliance.
3. Seal tile joints with silicone rubber on tiled worktops.

## Connecting the appliance - figure 9

- Verify the voltage on the rating plate.
- Connect only according to the wiring diagram in the original PDF.
- Conductor colours: `BN` brown, `BU` blue, `GN/YE` yellow-green, `BK` black, `GY` grey.
- Depending on the connection type, the factory-supplied terminal bridges may need to be rearranged. A bridge may need shortening and insulation may need removal to insert a bridge connecting two conductors. This work is for an authorised specialist only.

After connection, confirm that the appliance is ready for operation. If `U400`, `E0513`, or `E` appears on the display, disconnect the appliance from the power supply and have the cable connection checked.

## Removing the appliance

1. Disconnect the appliance from the mains supply.
2. Push the hob upward from below.

> **Caution:** Do not attempt to remove the hob by levering it out from above; this can damage the appliance.
